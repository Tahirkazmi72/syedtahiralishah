Bio

Syed Tahir Ali Shah is a Ph.D. student in mechanical engineering at the University of Aveiro, Portugal, working under the AM2R—Mobilizing Agenda for Business Innovation in the Two-Wheel Sector. He holds a bachelor's and a master's degree in computer science from Karakoram International University and Iqra University, Pakistan. His research focuses on emotion-aware, IoT-integrated mobility systems. He has authored and co-authored several scientific papers, including published and submitted work in areas such as wearable-driven smart mobility, embedded machine learning, affective computing, and IoT-based physiological monitoring.

Software

  • MariaBike: Real-Time E-Bike Dashboard with BLE and MQTT Integration
    Version 0.1, Android/iOS, 2025
    Universidade de Aveiro

MariaBike is a cutting-edge smart e-bike platform developed at the University of Aveiro as part of mobility innovation initiatives. It integrates physiological signals from wearables into embedded mobility solutions, enhancing the riding experience through emotion-aware IoT systems. Featured in events like the Regional Mobility Innovation Forum and EIT Urban Mobility RIS Winter Schools, MariaBike represents a proof-of-concept for human-centered IoT mobility.
