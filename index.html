About Me

Syed Tahir Ali Shah is a multidisciplinary PhD researcher working at the intersection of Mechanical Engineering, Computer Science, and embedded Internet-of-Things (IoT) systems. He is currently a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Aveiro (Portugal) and holds a PhD fellowship (BDANA track) supported by Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia (FCT).

With a solid background in Computer Science, his work bridges hardware–software integration, wearable sensing, and edge intelligence. His current research focuses on developing emotion-aware and physiology-aware mobility systems, using data from wearable devices (e.g., heart rate and motion signals) together with embedded machine learning models to support safer, more adaptive, and human-centred smart mobility solutions.

Overall, his work promotes the next generation of intelligent systems that respond to human needs in real time, combining engineering design, data science, and embedded computing.
